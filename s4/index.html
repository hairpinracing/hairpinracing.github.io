How It Works

Each team has 2 drivers. Each race runs one class pairing — either Gr1–Gr2 or Gr3–Gr4. Both teammates race every week, one in each class within that pairing: in a Gr1–Gr2 race, one drives Gr1 and the other drives Gr2. Teams decide internally who takes which class.

"Teams change driver class" markers in the schedule indicate the points where teammates swap roles within the pairing, so each driver covers all four classes by end of R4 and again by end of R9.

Sully won his class every single round from R1 through R8 — then posted "anyone available to fill in tonight?" hours before Brands Hatch and finished dead last in Gr4. He still leads the driver standings, but the margin has never been this thin.

Season Recap
Three Different Leaders
287 · 283 · 278 — 9 points cover the final podium
The championship changed hands twice: Heavy Sandbags led R1–R3, 911 Operators took over for five straight rounds (R4–R8), and Tail Pipes and Boners seized the top spot in R9 — then held on through the finale to become champions.
R10
Season Finale
Champions By 4 Points
39 pts at Alsace · won the title by the smallest margin yet
Heading into the winner-take-all finale, Tail Pipes and Boners led by just 2. iCrazy and Ncardw11 delivered 39 points at Alsace Village — enough to hold off Heavy Sandbags' 37 and seal the championship, 287 to 283.
R10
Too Little, Too Late
911's Best Round Comes One Round Short
40 pts · the finale's outright round win · finished 3rd overall
911 Operators actually won the finale outright — Sully and Leadfoot GT combined for 40 points, their best round of the season. It wasn't enough: the 3-point collapse at Brands Hatch in R9 had already cost them the title.
R9

A qualifying mishap at Brands Hatch left Leadfoot GT crashed into a wall before the race even started; he posted publicly that Sully should "find a new teammate," then walked it back a day later, citing a brutal week outside the sim.

By The Numbers

  • 4 Tail Pipes and Boners won the title by just 4 points over Heavy Sandbags, 287 to 283 — the closest finish of any Hairpin multi-class season to date.
  • 9/10 Sully won his class in 9 of the season's 10 rounds. The only blemish was R9, the round right before the finale — where he still bounced back to win Gr3 at Alsace.
  • 2× The championship lead changed hands twice. Heavy Sandbags (R1–R3) → 911 Operators (R4–R8) → Tail Pipes and Boners (R9 onward). Three different teams topped the standings this season.
  • 44 911 Operators' R4 score is the highest single-round team total of the season — built almost entirely on Leadfoot GT's Gr4 win at Fuji. Their 40-point finale (R10) came close, but arrived one round too late.
  • 9 Points separate 1st through 3rd in the final team standings — the tightest podium finish in league history.
  • 4/8 Only half the grid ever won a round outright all season — Tail Pipes and Boners (4 wins), 911 Operators (3), Heavy Sandbags (2), and The Lap Dancers (1). The other four teams never topped a single round, which is exactly why the final standings split into a tight top 3 and a separate, much wider pack behind them.
