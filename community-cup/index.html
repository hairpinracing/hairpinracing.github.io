Season 1

● Announced July 21, 2026 · First Race July 26

No standings. No pressure.
Just whatever the week's host feels like building.

Community Cup is Hairpin Racing's newest series — a deliberately casual, low-stakes break from the grind of a full championship. Every week a different registered driver designs and hosts the race: their car, their track, their rules. There's no points system, no fixed number of rounds, and no defined end date — organizer Pawl put it simply: "we'll race for as long as we have people involved and until everyone has built their own race." Eight drivers are in so far, with Round 1 (hosted by Pawl himself) set for Sunday, July 26.

02

How It Works

01
A New Host Every Week
Each round is designed by a different registered driver, taking turns in signup order. The host picks the car, track, and rules for their week and submits the details 48 hours before race day.
02
No Standings, No Points
This is not a championship. No scoring system has been set up, and none is planned — it's built purely to give the league a low-key, no-stakes night to look forward to.
03
Runs As Long As People Want
No fixed season length or end date. Per organizer Pawl, it continues "as long as we have people involved" — informally, until every registered driver has had a turn hosting.

04

Rules Established So Far

Most rules are set by each week's host. A few general guardrails have come up in early discussion:

Confirmed

  • Tuning and setups are allowed — Round 1 is explicitly a tuning race
  • Races run roughly 40–50 minutes as a rough guideline, but hosts can vary this or run by lap count instead

Not Allowed

  • No round can require a specific Legends car

Everything else — BoP, damage, assists, grid format — is set individually by each week's host and isn't known until they post it.
