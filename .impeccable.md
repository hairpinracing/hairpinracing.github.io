## Design Context

### Users
GT7 sim racers in a private Discord community. They access the site to check race results, standings, schedules, and registration status — mostly on desktop (checking before/after race sessions), occasionally on mobile. They're engaged fans of the league, not casual visitors. They want data fast and want it to feel like a real motorsport championship, not a hobbyist website.

### Brand Personality
**Gritty · Precise · Alive** — the feeling of a timing screen at a real circuit: dense data, no decoration for its own sake, every element there because it earns its place. Not corporate. Not polished to the point of sterility. Raw where rawness suits it.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-primary editorial with motorsport data-density. Anton (display) + Archivo (body) + Azeret Mono (data) — a trio that reads as deliberate and purposeful without veering into tryhard "racing game UI." Accent system is semantic: red = S1 / completed / intensity, yellow-gold = S2 / active / upcoming. Both accents are warm — deliberately avoiding the cold cyan/purple AI palette. Radial ambient gradients at very low opacity (4–12%) provide depth without glassmorphism.

**Reference feel**: F1 timing screens, Motorsport.com race archives, Le Mans results boards.
**Anti-references**: Generic esports sites (neon glows, particle effects), AI SaaS dashboards (card grids with icons, gradient text), Discord bots with embed cards.

### Design Principles
1. **Data is the decoration** — standings tables, championship boxes, and schedule rows are the visual centerpiece; don't bury them under chrome
2. **Typography carries hierarchy** — Anton at display scale vs Archivo at body vs Azeret Mono for numbers; weight and size do the work, not color alone
3. **Every animation earns its moment** — scroll reveals, marquee, header rise; no micro-interaction unless it communicates state
4. **Semantic color not decorative color** — red = intensity/completion, yellow = live/upcoming; don't use accent colors as arbitrary decoration
5. **Light mode is a first-class citizen** — the site works in both themes; dark is the default because GT7 is played in a dark room, but light mode must be clean, not an afterthought
